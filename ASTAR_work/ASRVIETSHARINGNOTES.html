  1. Study of Jonathan Hui article (summary)

https://medium.com/@jonathan_hui/speech-recognition-phonetics-d761ea1710c0 

Phonetics:

Sound can be voiced or voiceless (no vocal cord vibration)

Consonant are made by constriction and articulation

Vowel are voiced, made by height, backness, lip roundedness

Phoneme: the hidden state

Phone: acoustic realization of phonemes

Allophone: phones w diff acoustic but same phoneme

Process of acoustic generation in human (https://miro.medium.com/max/700/1*B3_3r18GDN2H247ViznovQ.png)

Hearing is non linear: the Cochlear are stiffer at front (high f) and softer towards the coil , send signal via the basilar membrane

https://jonathan-hui.medium.com/speech-recognition-feature-extraction-mfcc-plp-5455f5a69dd9 

Feature extraction in MFCC

*mfcc removes F0 (pitch)

AD: 8/16kHz sample

Preemphasis to boost energy in high f region (how to do this?)

Window size: 25ms w 10ms space to capture a phone, use Hamming/Hanning window to prevent sudden cutoff at the edge (they differ only by steepness of their grad 0.416-0.5)

DFT on window -> square it for power spectrum and then mel filterbank (series of triangular bandpass filter with widening base to simulate log growth)

Output from each bank slot is Mel binning

Until here there’s 2 steps left: remove the F0 information (the pitch) and makes the extracted features independent of others.

Cepstrum: IDFT: separate source (vocal cord) and filter (articulation) to separate pitch F0 from formants https://miro.medium.com/max/700/1*DnlhL9s3ogOosZBzvVU5Cw.jpeg by we takes the first 12 cepstral values (discard middle and far right), which also are orthogonal by DCT*

Delta: 13th feat is Energy, next 13 is transition (delta) and next 13 is delta-delta

CMVN: The mean and variance are computed with the feature value j over all the frames in a single utterance ISSUE: if the audio clip is short, this may not be reliable. Instead, we may compute the average and variance values based on speakers, or even over the entire training dataset. MFCC is not very robust against noise
